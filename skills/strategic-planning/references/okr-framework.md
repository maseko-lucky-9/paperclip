## OKR Writing Rules

### Good Key Results

A well-written KR is:
- **Measurable**: Has a number or a binary yes/no outcome
- **Timeboxed**: Achieved or not by end of quarter — not ongoing
- **Outcome-focused**: Describes a result, not an activity

| Bad KR (activity) | Good KR (outcome) |
|-------------------|--------------------|
| "Send proposals to 5 clients" | "5 qualified proposals sent, 2 accepted" |
| "Work on government tender" | "Submit 1 compliant tender by [date]" |
| "Improve cash flow" | "Maintain 3-month cash buffer (>R75k) at quarter-end" |
| "Learn Kubernetes" | "Deploy 1 client workload on MicroK8s, delivered and signed off" |

### Key Result Hierarchy by Objective Type

**Revenue Objective** — example KRs:
- Total revenue invoiced ≥ R[X]k by end of quarter
- Average effective rate ≥ R[X]/hour across all active projects
- Outstanding receivables <R[X]k at quarter-end

**Pipeline Objective** — example KRs:
- [X] new qualified opportunities in CRM by mid-quarter
- [X] proposals submitted by end of quarter
- Pipeline weighted value ≥ R[X]k

**Capability Objective** — example KRs:
- [Service line] delivered to 1 paying client by end of quarter
- [Certification] achieved by [date]
- Agent skill deployed and used on 2+ real tasks

**Compliance Objective** — example KRs:
- B-BBEE EME affidavit renewed and uploaded to CSD
- SARS tax clearance current (expiry >[date])
- CIPC annual return filed and confirmed
- Provisional tax payment made by [deadline]

## Quarterly Capacity Budget

Use this alongside OKRs to check feasibility before committing.

```
Available hours this quarter:
  Working days: ___ days × 8 hrs = ___ hrs total

Allocation:
  Billable delivery (60%):     ___ hrs  →  Revenue ceiling @ R___/hr = R___
  Sales & marketing (15%):     ___ hrs
  Operations & admin (10%):    ___ hrs
  Learning & development (10%): ___ hrs
  Buffer (5%):                 ___ hrs

Active project hours committed: ___ hrs
Remaining billable budget:      ___ hrs
Pipeline hours required:        ___ hrs
```

If pipeline hours > remaining billable budget: de-prioritise or defer. Do not over-commit.
