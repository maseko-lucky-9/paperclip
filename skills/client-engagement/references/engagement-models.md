# Engagement Models — Decision Framework and Contract Checklist

**Prudentia Digital** | Updated: 2026-01-01

---

## 1. Engagement Model Selection Framework

Use this decision tree when a new opportunity is being scoped. Start at the top and work down.

```
Is the scope clearly defined and unlikely to change?
├── YES → Is the total value > R50,000?
│         ├── YES → Fixed Price
│         └── NO  → Day Rate (short engagement)
└── NO  → Is there ongoing advisory / embedded need?
          ├── YES → Monthly Retainer
          └── NO  → Hourly (ad-hoc / unpredictable)
```

### When Each Model Wins

**Fixed Price** — use when:
- Requirements are documented and signed off before quoting.
- Client has a defined budget and procurement approval.
- Scope can be bounded with a clear in/out list.
- You can estimate effort with < 20% variance.

**Retainer** — use when:
- Client needs continuous access to expertise over 3+ months.
- Work volume is variable but the relationship has high strategic value.
- You want predictable recurring revenue.
- Client would otherwise hire a full-time resource (fractional model wins here).

**Day Rate** — use when:
- Short-duration engagement (1–10 days).
- Client is buying your time for workshops, on-site delivery, or facilitation.
- Project is well-defined but too small for fixed-price overhead.

**Hourly** — use when:
- Engagement is highly unpredictable in scope or cadence.
- Client is in the evaluation phase and not ready to commit.
- Support or maintenance with irregular demand.
- As a fallback when retainer negotiation stalls.

## 2. Engagement Model Comparison

| Attribute | Hourly | Day Rate | Retainer | Fixed Price |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue predictability | Low | Medium | High | Medium-High |
| Scope risk | High | Medium | Medium | Low (if SOW tight) |
| Admin overhead | High (timesheets) | Medium | Low | Medium (milestones) |
| Client budget certainty | Low | Medium | High | High |
| Relationship depth | Low | Medium | High | Medium |
| Upsell potential | Low | Low | High | Medium |
| Suitable for government | Low | High | Low | High |
| Cash flow impact | Variable | Variable | Predictable | Front-loaded |

## 3. Contract Checklist

Use this checklist for every new engagement before work begins.

### Pre-Contract

- [ ] Verbal scope alignment confirmed with key stakeholder.
- [ ] Budget range confirmed (avoid full proposal on unbudgeted opportunities).
- [ ] Decision-maker identified — who signs the contract?
- [ ] Procurement process understood (direct, 3 quotes, tender, preferred vendor).
- [ ] B-BBEE certificate attached to proposal if required.
- [ ] CSD registration confirmed for government clients.

### Documents Required

- [ ] **SOW signed** — scope, deliverables, timeline, exclusions, acceptance criteria.
- [ ] **MSA signed** (for multi-engagement clients) — IP, liability, payment terms, dispute resolution.
- [ ] **POPIA DPA signed** — if personal information will be accessed or processed.
- [ ] **NDA signed** — mutual preferred.
- [ ] **Purchase order received** (government / large enterprise) — match PO number on all invoices.

### SOW Must-Haves

- [ ] In-scope list (explicit, numbered).
- [ ] Out-of-scope list (explicit — protects against scope creep claims).
- [ ] Deliverable definitions with acceptance criteria.
- [ ] Timeline with milestones.
- [ ] Payment schedule and amounts.
- [ ] Change request process reference.
- [ ] Client dependencies (what Prudentia Digital needs from the client, and by when).
- [ ] Assumptions (if any assumption is wrong, the SOW is re-scoped).

### IP and Legal

- [ ] IP ownership clause: work-product vests in client on full payment.
- [ ] Pre-existing IP clause: Prudentia Digital tools/frameworks remain our IP; license granted.
- [ ] Liability cap: 3 months' fees or R50,000 minimum.
- [ ] Exclusion of consequential/indirect damages.
- [ ] Client indemnity for third-party claims arising from client data/instructions.
- [ ] Governing law: South Africa. Jurisdiction: [Thulani's city of operation].

### Payment

- [ ] Payment terms stated on invoice and in SOW.
- [ ] Banking details correct and on invoice template.
- [ ] Deposit invoice raised before work starts (fixed price and large retainers).
- [ ] Late payment interest clause included (Prime + 2% p.a.).
- [ ] VAT number on all invoices.

## 4. Government and SOE-Specific Requirements

Government engagements carry additional process requirements. Address all before quoting.

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| CSD Registration | Central Supplier Database — must be active and compliant |
| B-BBEE Certificate | Level 1 EME; attach to all submissions; must be < 12 months old |
| Tax Clearance | SARS Tax Compliance Status (TCS) — confirm status is "Good Standing" |
| Procurement threshold | < R500k = 3 written quotes; > R500k = open tender (PFMA rules) |
| Payment terms | PFMA mandates 30 days; budget for 60–90 days in practice |
| SLA requirements | State may require formal SLA with defined uptime / response time |
| Delegation of authority | Confirm the signatory has the authority to bind the entity |

## 5. Retainer Lifecycle Management

```
Month 1–2: Onboarding & establishing value
Month 3–4: Delivery rhythm established; capture quick wins
Month 5–6: Expansion conversation; surface adjacent needs
Month 6: Renewal conversation initiated (6 weeks before expiry)
Month 7: Renewal SOW / addendum signed; rate review applied
```

### Retainer Health Check (monthly, informal)

Ask yourself:
1. Is the client drawing on the included hours? (Under-use = cancellation risk.)
2. Did I deliver at least one clearly articulated piece of value this month?
3. Have I communicated proactively — or only when the client reached out?
4. Is the client aware of adjacent services Prudentia Digital could provide?

## 6. Proposal Structure

Standard proposal for a commercial engagement:

1. **Executive Summary** — 1 paragraph. Problem, solution, outcome. No jargon.
2. **Understanding of Requirements** — show you listened; reference their language.
3. **Proposed Approach** — methodology, phases, key activities.
4. **Deliverables** — numbered list with brief description of each.
5. **Timeline** — Gantt or milestone table.
6. **Investment** — fees by phase or total, payment schedule, what's excluded.
7. **About Prudentia Digital** — 1 paragraph. Relevant experience, B-BBEE status, Thulani's bio.
8. **Next Steps** — clear call to action: "Sign the attached SOW to initiate."
9. **Attachments** — B-BBEE certificate, relevant case study or testimonial, SOW draft.

**Proposal validity**: 30 days from date issued. State this on the cover page.
