# Report Templates — Prudentia Digital

Full document templates for client-facing and internal reports. See SKILL.md for guidance on cadence, audience calibration, and when to use each format.

---

## 1. Weekly Status Report (Email Body — Short Form)

The default weekly client-facing update. Kept to under 300 words. This is the email body, not an attachment.

```
Subject: [Client Name] — Weekly Update | [W/C Date]

Hi [Name],

Here's a quick update on [project name] for the week of [date range].

STATUS: [On Track / At Risk / Off Track]
[One sentence explaining the status rating — only include if At Risk or Off Track]

COMPLETED THIS WEEK
- [Plain-English description of completed work. No issue IDs.]
- [Item 2]
- [Item 3 — list only meaningful completions; don't pad]

IN PROGRESS
- [Task description] — targeting completion by [date]
- [Task description] — [note any dependency on client action]

BLOCKERS / ACTIONS NEEDED FROM YOUR SIDE
[List specific requests with deadlines. Be explicit: "I need X from Y by Z to avoid
slipping milestone."]
[If none: "No blockers this week — no action needed from your side."]

NEXT WEEK
- [Priority 1]
- [Priority 2]
- [Priority 3]

MILESTONE TRACKER
| Milestone | Target Date | Status |
|-----------|------------|--------|
| [Milestone 1] | [Date] | [On Track / At Risk] |
| [Milestone 2] | [Date] | [On Track] |

Let me know if you have any questions.

Kind regards,
Thulani Maseko
Prudentia Digital
[mobile] | thulani@prudentiadigital.co.za
```

## 2. Monthly Progress Report (Document — 1-2 Pages)

Sent at the end of each calendar month for all retainer and medium-to-large fixed-price engagements. Formatted as a PDF attachment to an email.

### Email Cover Note

```
Subject: [Client Name] — Monthly Progress Report | [Month Year]

Hi [Name],

Please find attached the monthly progress report for [project name] covering [month].

The highlights for this month: [2-3 bullet summary — copy from the Executive Summary].

No action is required unless noted in the "Decisions Required" section of the report.

Kind regards,
Thulani Maseko
Prudentia Digital
```

## 5. Post-Project Retrospective Report (Internal)

Internal document only — not shared with clients. Produced within 5 business days of project closure. Kept in the project file.

```
POST-PROJECT RETROSPECTIVE
Project: [Name]
Client: [Name]
Period: [Start date] — [End date]
Engagement type: [Fixed-price / Retainer / Tender]
Total value: R[amount]
Hours contracted: [X] | Hours delivered: [X] | Variance: [+/- X%]
Date of retrospective: [Date]

---

WHAT WENT WELL
1. [Specific item — not generic. "Client gave clear, timely feedback in sprint reviews" is good.
   "Good communication" is not useful.]
2. [Item]
3. [Item]

WHAT DIDN'T WORK
1. [Honest observation. "Underestimated the database migration complexity by 40%" is actionable.
   "There were some delays" is not.]
2. [Item]
3. [Item]

ROOT CAUSES (top 2 issues)
Issue 1: [Title]
  Root cause: [Why did this actually happen? Use 5 Whys if useful.]
  Contributing factor: [What made it worse?]

Issue 2: [Title]
  Root cause: [...]
  Contributing factor: [...]

ACTIONS FOR NEXT ENGAGEMENT
1. [Specific change — verb-first, owner is Thulani: "Build 20% estimation buffer for all
   database migration tasks." Not "be more careful."]
2. [Action]
3. [Action — max 3; more than 3 won't be actioned]

CLIENT RELATIONSHIP HEALTH AT CLOSE
Overall: [Strong / Neutral / Strained]
Satisfaction signals: [What evidence do you have? Testimonial offered? Repeat inquiry? Complained?]
Renewal potential: [High / Medium / Low / None]
Referral potential: [High / Medium / Low]
Action: [Follow-up in [X months]? Add to case study list? Avoid?]

FINANCIAL OUTCOME
Revenue: R[amount]
Estimated cost (Thulani hours × effective hourly cost): R[amount]
Gross margin: R[amount] ([X%])
[Note if margin was significantly above or below target, and why]

CASE STUDY POTENTIAL
[Yes / No] — [If yes: brief note on what the case study angle would be]
```
