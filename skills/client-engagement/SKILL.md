# Skill: Client Engagement

**Role**: CEO Agent — Thulani Maseko / Prudentia Digital
**Context**: Solo SA tech consultancy. B-BBEE Level 1 EME. Primary markets: SME, enterprise, and government in South Africa. All rates in ZAR. Operates under South African law (Companies Act, POPIA, NCA where applicable).

---

## 1. Engagement Models

### Overview

Four canonical engagement types. Choose based on client maturity, budget predictability, and project clarity. Never default to hourly — it caps revenue and commoditises expertise.

| Model | Rate Range | Best Fit | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hourly | R850–R1,200/hr | Ad-hoc advisory, support retainers with unpredictable draw-down | Revenue unpredictability; scope creep risk highest |
| Daily (Day Rate) | R6,500–R9,000/day | Short engagements, workshops, on-site delivery sprints | Requires defined deliverable per day or week |
| Monthly Retainer | R15,000–R40,000/mo | Ongoing advisory, fractional CTO/SA, embedded support | Must define included hours and scope; add overage clause |
| Fixed Price | R50,000–R500,000 | Clearly scoped projects: migrations, platform builds, audits | Requires watertight SOW; scope change = CR |

### Hourly (R850–R1,200/hr)

- **R850/hr**: SME clients, straightforward tasks, relationship building engagements.
- **R1,000/hr**: Standard commercial rate. Architecture review, solution design, technical due diligence.
- **R1,200/hr**: Crisis response, last-minute availability, specialist niche (e.g., security architecture, multi-cloud design).
- **Minimum billing unit**: 2 hours. No sub-hour billing on standalone engagements.
- **Travel time**: Bill at 50% of applicable rate for travel exceeding 45 minutes one-way.

### Day Rate (R6,500–R9,000/day)

- **R6,500/day**: SME, non-technical stakeholders, discovery workshops.
- **R7,500/day**: Standard commercial. On-site delivery, implementation sprints, training facilitation.
- **R9,000/day**: Enterprise, boardroom-level advisory, government engagements with high-stakes deliverables.
- **Day definition**: 8 billable hours (09:00–17:00 with 1-hour break). Overtime beyond 8 hours billed at R1,200/hr.
- **Minimum engagement**: 2 days for day-rate bookings.

### Monthly Retainer (R15,000–R40,000/mo)

- **R15,000/mo**: Light-touch advisory. Up to 10 hours/month. Email + async comms. 1 monthly check-in call.
- **R25,000/mo**: Fractional SA. Up to 20 hours/month. Weekly sync. Architecture reviews and vendor calls included.
- **R40,000/mo**: Embedded fractional CTO/SA. Up to 40 hours/month. Slack/Teams access, incident response coverage, board-level reporting.
- **Overage clause**: Hours above included allocation billed at applicable hourly rate (R1,000/hr standard).
- **Rollover policy**: Unused hours do NOT roll over. Make this explicit in the contract.
- **Notice period**: 30 calendar days written notice to cancel retainer.

### Fixed Price (R50,000–R500,000)

- **R50k–R150k**: Audits, assessments, proof-of-concept builds, architecture blueprints.
- **R150k–R300k**: Platform migrations, cloud landing zone design and implementation, full solution delivery.
- **R300k–R500k**: Enterprise engagements, multi-phase programmes, bespoke platform development.
- **Payment structure**: 50% upfront, 25% at midpoint milestone, 25% on final delivery acceptance.
- **Fixed price requires**: Signed SOW with explicit in-scope / out-of-scope list before work begins.
- **Change requests**: Any scope change triggers a formal CR with cost and timeline impact documented.

## 2. Rate Card Application

### Client Segmentation

| Segment | Rate Positioning | Key Considerations |
|---|---|---|
| SME (turnover < R50m) | Lower band of each tier | Budget-sensitive; emphasise ROI and risk reduction; shorter contracts |
| Commercial Enterprise (R50m–R1bn) | Mid-to-upper band | Focus on reliability, SLA, scalability; procurement processes apply |
| Listed / Large Corp (> R1bn) | Upper band + premium conditions | Slow procurement; require B-BBEE cert upfront; NDA standard |
| Government / SOE | Day rate or fixed price; upper band | Long payment cycles (30–90 days); require CSD registration; strict procurement rules |
| Non-profit / NGO | Negotiable; 20–30% discount available | Case-by-case; never below cost; document as social investment if applicable |

### Premium Conditions (charge upper band or add surcharge)

- Engagement start within 5 business days of signing.
- Weekend or public holiday work (+50% surcharge).
- Deliverable turnaround under 48 hours (+25% rush surcharge).
- Engagements requiring NDA with aggressive IP clauses.
- Client has changed SA at least once in 12 months (churn risk; price for it).

### Discount Conditions (move to lower band only)

- Multi-year retainer commitment (>12 months): up to 10% discount.
- Referral from existing client: one-time 5% discount on first invoice.
- Strategic partnership value (case-by-case; must be approved by Thulani before quoting).
- Never discount below lower band. Floor is R850/hr, R6,500/day, R15k/mo.

### B-BBEE Leverage

Prudentia Digital is a Level 1 EME contributor (135% procurement recognition). Lead with this for:
- Government and SOE procurement.
- Enterprise clients with B-BBEE scorecards (preferential procurement pillar).
- Tender submissions — always include B-BBEE certificate as a standard attachment.

## 3. Contract Essentials (South Africa)

### Core Documents Required Before Work Begins

1. **Signed Statement of Work (SOW)** — defines scope, deliverables, timeline, exclusions.
2. **Master Services Agreement (MSA)** — if multi-engagement client; governs all future SOWs.
3. **POPIA Data Processing Agreement (DPA)** — mandatory if you will access, process, or store personal information.
4. **Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)** — mutual preferred; one-sided NDAs acceptable on a case-by-case basis.

### IP Ownership

- **Default position**: All IP created during a fixed-price or retainer engagement vests in the client upon full payment.
- **Pre-existing IP**: Prudentia Digital retains ownership of any tools, frameworks, templates, or methodologies brought into the engagement. License granted to client for use within the project scope.
- **Open-source components**: Disclose in SOW; client accepts applicable OSS licences.
- **Never sign away pre-existing IP** without explicit written approval from Thulani and a compensating premium.

### Liability Caps

- **Standard cap**: Total fees paid in the 3 months preceding the claim.
- **Minimum floor**: R50,000 regardless of fees paid (for small retainers).
- **Exclude**: Indirect, consequential, or punitive damages. Explicitly disclaim loss of profits, loss of data, reputational harm.
- **Government engagements**: State may attempt to remove cap — resist; seek legal review if client insists.
- **Indemnity**: Client indemnifies Prudentia Digital against third-party claims arising from client-provided data or instructions.

### Payment Terms

| Client Type | Standard Terms | Acceptable Exception |
|---|---|---|
| SME | 14 days from invoice | 30 days max with written agreement |
| Commercial | 30 days from invoice | 45 days with 2% early payment discount offered |
| Government / SOE | 30 days (PFMA mandated) | 60–90 days in practice; factor into cash flow |

- **Late payment interest**: Prime + 2% per annum (currently ~13.75% p.a.) on overdue amounts. State this in all contracts.
- **Upfront deposits**: Always required for fixed-price engagements. 50% non-refundable if client cancels after SOW signing.
- **Invoicing cadence**: Retainers invoiced on the 1st of each month in advance. Time-and-materials invoiced weekly or bi-weekly.

### POPIA Compliance

- Any engagement touching personal information of SA data subjects requires a signed DPA.
- As operator, document: data categories processed, purpose, retention period, deletion process.
- Never store client personal data beyond project lifetime without explicit written consent.
- Report data breaches to the client within 72 hours of discovery; assist with POPIA Section 22 notification to Information Regulator if threshold met.

## 4. Scope Management

### Change Request Process

All scope changes — no matter how small — follow this process:

1. **Identify**: Client request falls outside signed SOW. Do not start work.
2. **Document**: Capture the request in writing (email summary acceptable as a precursor).
3. **Assess**: Estimate time, cost, and schedule impact within 2 business days.
4. **Issue CR**: Send formal Change Request document with: description, rationale, impact on timeline, cost, and any risks introduced.
5. **Sign-off**: Get written approval (email confirmation acceptable) before proceeding.
6. **Update SOW**: Attach signed CR as an addendum to the original SOW.

### Scope Creep Detection Signals

Watch for these patterns:
- "While you're at it, can you also..." (the most common vector).
- Verbal requests made during calls that bypass the documented process.
- Client invites Prudentia Digital into meetings outside the agreed engagement scope.
- Deliverable review meetings where requirements shift materially from the original brief.
- Timeline pressure from client that implies doing more work in the same period.

### Scope Creep Response Script

> "That's a good addition — it's outside the current SOW scope. I'll put together a quick Change Request so we can get it properly scoped and approved. It shouldn't take long to formalise. Does that work?"

Never say "it's fine, I'll just do it" — this sets a precedent and erodes margins.

## 5. Client Health Signals

### Green (Healthy Engagement)

- Invoices paid on time or early.
- Stakeholder responsiveness within 1 business day.
- Clear, constructive feedback on deliverables.
- Proactive sharing of context (strategy docs, org changes, upcoming projects).
- Verbal or written expressions of satisfaction.
- Referrals to other potential clients.

### Amber (Watch Closely)

- Invoice payment delayed more than 7 days without explanation.
- Reduced stakeholder responsiveness or frequent reschedules.
- Feedback becomes vague or evasive ("let's revisit next week").
- New internal stakeholder introduced who is unfamiliar with the engagement.
- Unusual silence after a major deliverable submission.
- Questions about competitor pricing or alternative providers.

### Red (Escalation Required)

- Invoice overdue by more than 21 days.
- Stakeholder stops responding entirely.
- Client signals budget freeze or restructuring.
- Deliverable rejected without substantive reason.
- Client attempts to renegotiate already-agreed terms.
- Legal or compliance queries directed at the engagement.

### Escalation Response (Red Signals)

1. Send a polite but direct written communication requesting a call within 48 hours.
2. Pause non-critical work if payment is overdue by 30+ days (state this in the MSA upfront).
3. Engage legal counsel if a formal dispute arises.
4. Document all communications from the point escalation is identified.

## 6. Upsell and Renewal

### Expansion Signals

- Client references a problem adjacent to the current engagement ("we also have this issue with X").
- Stakeholder expresses frustration with an internal team's capability.
- Client is about to undertake a procurement or vendor evaluation.
- Deliverable success generates executive visibility for the engagement.
- Client team headcount growing — indicates scaling pain ahead.

### Retainer Renewal Timing

- **Initiate renewal conversation**: 6 weeks before expiry for retainers under 6 months; 8 weeks for longer retainers.
- **Renewal review format**: 30-minute call covering: outcomes delivered, value realised, upcoming priorities, retainer scope adjustments.
- **Rate adjustment**: Review annually. CPI + 5% is a reasonable floor for retainer renewal increases. For significant scope expansion, reprice fully.
- **Renewal document**: Issue a new SOW or retainer addendum — never continue on a verbal basis after expiry.

### Multi-Service Bundling

| Bundle | Components | Positioning |
|---|---|---|
| Advisory + Implementation | Retainer + fixed-price project | Ensures continuity; client gets strategic and delivery value from one partner |
| Audit + Roadmap | Fixed-price assessment + follow-on retainer | Audit surfaces problems; retainer monetises the remediation |
| Fractional CTO + Delivery Sprint | Monthly retainer + day-rate sprints | Scales with client need; predictable base + variable upside |

- Bundle pricing: Offer 5–8% combined discount on bundled services (total, not per service).
- Always present bundles as risk reduction and continuity, not cost-cutting.
